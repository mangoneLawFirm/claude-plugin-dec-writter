## HARD FAILS — Never Allow in Final Declaration

| Rule | Prohibited | Correct |
|------|-----------|---------|
| First-person singular | "us/we/our" for victim group | "I/me/my" exclusively |
| Firing fear | "afraid they would fire me" | Fear of immigration/harm/destitution |
| English-only | ANY Spanish text | All translated to English |
| Sexual violence language | "sexually abused/assault" | "sexual slave/servitude" |
| VAWA filter | Trafficking para without Means→Ends | Every para connects force/fraud to labor/servitude |
| Isolation integrity | Third-party exit advice, family visits, dual-persona | Exclude or reframe as control |

## Section Word Count Targets

| Section | Target | FAIL if |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Physical Presence | 150-300 words, 1-2 ¶ | >350 words or 3+ ¶ |
| Extreme Hardship | 250-400 words, 3 ¶ max | >500 words or 5+ ¶ |
| Trafficking | 1,200-2,200 words | Shorter than any other section |
| Total Declaration | 2,500-3,500 words | >4,000 words |
| Background | STRICTLY 2-3 ¶ | 3+ ¶ without attorney override |

## Trafficking Section Quick-Check

- First ¶ = process/setup only (no exploitation)
- Agreement around PROMISES not victim's needs
- Every ¶ connects MEANS to ENDS
- Enforcement mechanisms specify HOW (specific consequences)
- Active voice — trafficker as subject
- Max 2-3 ¶ open with trafficker's name
- Servitude contrast present
- Escape barriers explicitly addressed
- 3-5 conversational authenticity markers

## Required Legal Elements

- EH must cite INA § 101(a)(15)(T)(i)(IV) and 8 C.F.R. § 214.209
- EH must include country conditions paragraph
- PP must state "directly connected to the trafficking"
- Introduction has NO heading

## Good vs Bad Examples (Quick Reference)

**Trafficking Detail:**
- BAD: "She forced me to cook and clean."
- GOOD: "Most days, I came home late and tired, and I still had to cook dinner from scratch — rice, beans, meat, tortillas — and prepare food for the next day..."

**VAWA vs Trafficking:**
- BAD: "She insulted me and made me feel small."
- GOOD: "She insulted me constantly to keep me obedient. If I resisted cooking, she threatened to call immigration."

**Enforcement:**
- BAD: "I had seen what they did to people who complained."
- GOOD: "I had seen the leader beat a man who refused to work until he could not stand up."

**Compliance:**
- BAD: "My goal was to keep the peace."
- GOOD: "I was terrified of what she would do. She had threatened to call police and have me deported."
