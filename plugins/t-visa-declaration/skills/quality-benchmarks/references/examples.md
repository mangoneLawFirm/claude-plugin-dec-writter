# Good vs Bad Examples and Prohibited Words Quick-Reference

## Good vs Bad Examples (From Real Documents)

### Trafficking Detail

**BAD (AI Draft v0.7):**
> She forced me to cook lunch and dinner, and she also made me clean the house, bathrooms, and floors when I got home.

**GOOD (Human Final):**
> Most days, I came home late and tired, and I still had to cook. I would make dinner from scratch, like rice, beans, meat or chicken, tortillas, and eggs, depending on what we had. I also had to prepare food for the next day, like packing leftovers or getting things ready so she wouldn't complain in the morning. On top of that, I had to wash dishes, clean the kitchen, take out the trash, and leave everything "right," because if she saw a mess, she would start yelling.

**Why it matters:** The specific foods and tasks demonstrate the burden of forced labor. Generic "cook and clean" could describe normal household duties. The specifics make it clear this was exploitation.

### Verbal Abuse

**BAD (AI Draft v0.7):**
> she yelled at me with bad words and said, "Start cooking, you do nothing"

**GOOD (Human Final):**
> She would say things like, "You don't do anything," "Get up and make food," and "You're useless." She would curse at me and talk to me like I was nothing.

**Why it matters:** Multiple specific insults show a pattern of degradation, not a single incident.

### Conclusion Tone

**BAD (AI Draft v0.7):**
> I respectfully ask USCIS to let me remain in the United States and to approve my T-Visa application so I can live safely and rebuild my life here.

**GOOD (Human Final):**
> I beg you to grant me the opportunity to stay legally in the U.S. so that I can seek the psychological and emotional help I need, heal from the trauma I've experienced, and finally have a dignified life.

**Why it matters:** "I beg you" is emotionally direct and vulnerable. "I respectfully ask" is formal and distancing.

### Extreme Hardship — Handling Missing Data

**BAD (AI Draft v0.7):**
> [DETAILS NEEDED: current Mexico/Tamaulipas country conditions facts, including recent crime/kidnapping/homicide statistics and names of criminal organizations active in/around Matamoros.]

**GOOD (Human Final):**
> In Mexico, especially in the region where I am from, access to mental health treatment is extremely limited, with almost no specialized services for victims of trafficking or abuse. People often face stigma, are dismissed, or are left without care.

**Why it matters:** The human writer argues with available knowledge rather than leaving a placeholder. The statement works without statistics because the argument is grounded in treatment access, not crime rates.

### Moral Character — Immigration Violations

**BAD (AI Draft v0.7):**
> I regret that I stayed in the United States without lawful status, and I understand that this is a serious problem. At that time, I felt like I had to keep working to survive...

**GOOD (Human Final):**
> I first entered the United States lawfully with my Border Crossing Card. Over time, I stayed longer than I was allowed to, and I understand that was not permitted. I sincerely apologize for that. I did not stay to break the law. I stayed to survive and support myself through honest work. I respectfully ask you to excuse my immigration violation and allow me to remain in the United States, because my continued presence supports law enforcement cooperation...

**Why it matters:** "I sincerely apologize" takes ownership. Connecting the waiver to law enforcement cooperation gives USCIS a reason to grant it.

### VAWA-vs-Trafficking Framing (CRITICAL)

**BAD (reads as VAWA / domestic violence):**
> She insulted me and made me feel small, like nothing I did was enough. I would send my location because I was trying to avoid accusations and problems. It felt like I had to prove myself all the time.

**GOOD (reads as trafficking):**
> She insulted me constantly to keep me obedient. If I showed any resistance to cooking or cleaning after my shift, she would threaten to call immigration. She demanded I send my location to verify I was at work earning the money she required. Everything she did was to make sure I kept working and serving her.

**Why it matters:** USCIS adjudicates T-visas, not VAWA petitions. If the trafficking section reads as emotional abuse without connection to labor/servitude exploitation, the case fails regardless of how strong the facts are. Every paragraph must connect MEANS (force/threats) to ENDS (labor/money/servitude).

### Coerced Compliance (CRITICAL)

**BAD (sounds voluntary):**
> I would do those chores fast and right, because my goal was to keep the peace. My son stayed with her for about 15 days, and I told her it was okay as long as she took care of him.

**GOOD (sounds coerced):**
> I did those chores as fast as I could because I was terrified of what she would do if she found anything wrong. She had threatened to call police and have me deported, and I believed she would do it. My son had to stay with her because I had nowhere to take him — she had made sure I had no money and no alternatives. I wanted my son with me, but the circumstances she created made that impossible.

**Why it matters:** If the client sounds like they chose to comply or accepted a situation, the adjudicator concludes they were not trafficked. Every act of compliance must be framed as fear-driven.

## Prohibited Words Quick-Reference

### NEVER use these — always substitute:

| Prohibited | Use Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| subjected to | forced to, made to |
| exploitation | being used, being taken advantage of |
| experienced trauma | felt scared, was hurt |
| psychological abuse | threats, fear, being scared |
| perpetrator | person's name, he/she |
| utilized | used |
| coercive tactics | threats, pressure |
| confined to premises | not allowed to leave |
| sustained injuries | was hurt, got injured |
| endured hardship | suffered, went through hard times |
| commenced / initiated | started, began |
| subsequent to | after |
| prior to | before |
| residing at | living at |
| pursuant to | because of |
| aforementioned | the, this |
| subsequently | later, then |
| witnessed | saw |
| encountered | met |
| facilitated | helped |
| instances of | times when |
| on numerous occasions | many times |
| due to the fact that | because |
| confiscated | took |
| compelled to | forced to |
| smuggling | NEVER use |
| respectfully request (standalone) | respectfully and humbly ask, ask, beg |
| sexually abused | forced to serve as sexual slave, forced into sexual servitude |
| sexual abuse | sexual slavery, sexual servitude |
| sexual assault | forced sex, sexual slavery |
| sexually assaulted | forced himself/herself on me |
