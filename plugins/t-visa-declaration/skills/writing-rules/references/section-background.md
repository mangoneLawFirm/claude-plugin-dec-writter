# Background Section Instructions

## Format Rules
- **Paragraph count**: STRICTLY 2 paragraphs (no 3rd paragraph without explicit attorney override)
- **Numbering**: Continue from Introduction (typically paragraphs 2-3)
- **Heading**: No section heading

## Paragraph 1: Early Life & Vulnerability Foundation

**Required elements:**
- Origin country/region
- Family structure and composition
- Educational background/access
- Isolation indicators (geographic, social, linguistic)
- Lack of community/institutional support
- Immigration history prior to trafficking
- Any childhood vulnerabilities that facilitated trafficking

**Vulnerability Test**: Every fact must show WHY client was susceptible to being trafficked. Facts must demonstrate absence of protective factors.

**Critical Rule**: NO INTENT TO REMAIN — absolutely no language suggesting permanent intent to stay in any location. Avoid: "I wanted to make my life there," "I planned to stay," "I dreamed of building a future."

**Scope**: Anything predating the trafficking situation — family dynamics, education gaps, economic desperation, prior abuse, etc.

## Paragraph 2: Context at Point of Contact with Trafficker

**Key Decision: WHERE was trafficker met?**

### IF TRAFFICKER MET IN US:
- Include only: children's details (ages, names, whereabouts) and immigration context at entry
- Do NOT introduce the trafficker as a character
- Do NOT establish US desperation/vulnerability
- No paragraph about US conditions/needs that made trafficking possible
- Focus: minimal context about client's situation when trafficker entered life

### IF TRAFFICKER MET BEFORE US ENTRY:
- How/where trafficking relationship began
- Age disparity between client and trafficker
- How dependency was established
- Pre-US abuse or coercion patterns
- What promises were made (false promises that lured client forward)
- Why client believed/agreed to travel or relationship
- Economic desperation only if directly tied to trafficker's false promise

**Vulnerability Connection**: End paragraph with clear linkage between vulnerability established in ¶1 and the specific circumstances that made client vulnerable to THIS trafficker's recruitment.

## Content Boundaries

**DO INCLUDE:**
- Family background, siblings, parents, economic status
- Education gaps or lack of schooling
- Community isolation
- Childhood abuse or prior exploitation
- Immigration history/visa status before trafficking
- Reasons for vulnerability to this specific recruitment

**DO NOT INCLUDE:**
- Transportation/journey details (belongs in Trafficking section)
- Specific trafficking tasks or exploitation (belongs in Trafficking)
- Physical abuse details (belongs in Trafficking)
- Financial coercion specifics (belongs in Trafficking)
- US conditions that made trafficking possible (unless trafficker met before US and must explain promises)

## Childhood Vulnerability Questions
When facts are available, incorporate:
- Did client experience prior abuse or exploitation?
- Was client separated from family?
- Did client have limited education?
- Was client in foster care, juvenile system, or institutional care?
- Did client have unstable housing?
- Did family struggle with substance abuse, domestic violence, mental health?
- Was client isolated due to immigration status?
- Did client have limited peer relationships or community ties?

## Connection to Trafficking
Background section must make clear WHY this particular client was vulnerable to being trafficked by this particular trafficker. The vulnerabilities established in Background make the trafficking in the next section credible and comprehensible.
