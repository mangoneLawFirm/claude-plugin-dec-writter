# Law Enforcement Section Instructions

## Format Rules
- **Paragraph count**: 1 paragraph only
- **Numbering**: Continue sequential numbering from Physical Presence section
- **Heading**: No section heading
- **Brevity**: Concise, focused section

## Required Elements (ALL must be present)

1. **Reporting status** — Has client reported to law enforcement? Yes/No/Partial?
2. **Willingness statement** — Client's current willingness to cooperate with investigations
3. **Altruistic motivation** — Why client is cooperating (if not solely for T visa)
4. **FBI letter date** — If FBI letter received, include the date
5. **Delayed cooperation explanation** — If client delayed reporting, briefly explain why (if applicable)

## Optional Elements (Include if Applicable)

- **Domestic violence arrest**: If client was arrested for DV (often a collateral consequence of trafficking), lead with trafficking-specific reporting to show priority
- **Prior police contact**: Brief mention of unsuccessful prior reports or barriers to reporting
- **Language/communication barriers**: Why reporting took time or why client needed help

---

## Standard Structure

### For Client Who Reported to Law Enforcement

"I reported the trafficking to [agency name] on [date]. I provided [brief description: detailed statements, evidence, names of co-victims, etc.]. Since then, I have cooperated fully with [agency] and provided [ongoing description: additional statements, court appearances, etc.]. I am willing to continue cooperating with any federal investigation. [Altruistic motivation: 'I want others to be safe,' 'I want [trafficker] to be prosecuted,' 'I want to protect other victims,' etc.]. [If applicable: On [date], I received a letter from the FBI confirming my cooperation.]"

### For Client Who Has Not Yet Reported

"I have not yet reported the trafficking to law enforcement. [Explanation: I was afraid, I did not know how, I did not trust police, etc.]. However, I am now willing to cooperate with law enforcement investigations. [Altruistic motivation]. I understand that cooperation with law enforcement is important for the protection of other victims and for holding [trafficker] accountable."

### For Client with Prior Report Rejection or Language Barriers

"[First attempt/barrier]: [Brief detail]. [Second attempt/outcome]: [Brief detail]. I finally reported the trafficking to [agency] on [date]. I have been cooperating since then. [Altruistic motivation and willingness]."

---

## Critical Rules

### Altruistic Motivation Required
Client must demonstrate motivation beyond T visa.

**NOT ACCEPTABLE**: "I reported because I need a visa."

**ACCEPTABLE**:
- "I reported because I want [trafficker] held accountable"
- "I want to protect other victims from experiencing what I experienced"
- "I felt a moral obligation to come forward so others would be safe"
- "I wanted justice, not just for me but for [co-victims]"
- Combination: "I reported because I want [trafficker] prosecuted, and I hope that my cooperation will help law enforcement stop him from harming others."

### FBI Letter Date Mandatory
If client has FBI cooperation letter, include the date.

**Example**: "On [specific date], I received a letter from the Federal Bureau of Investigation confirming my cooperation with their investigation into [trafficker's name/case]."

### Delayed Cooperation Explanation
If client delayed reporting, briefly acknowledge and explain.

**NOT ACCEPTABLE**: No explanation of delay.

**ACCEPTABLE** (brief explanations):
- "I was afraid of deportation"
- "I did not speak English well and did not know how to report"
- "I did not trust police"
- "I did not realize what [trafficker] did was a crime"
- "I thought I was the one who would be blamed or arrested"
- "I was still under [trafficker's] control and was afraid of the consequences if I reported"

**Examples in context**:
- "I did not report the trafficking immediately because I was afraid of being deported. It was not until [person/organization] explained that I could seek help without being deported that I found the courage to report."
- "I was afraid to go to the police because [trafficker] had told me they would arrest me. When [person/organization] assured me that I was a victim and could receive protection, I reported."

### DV Arrest + FBI Report: Lead with Trafficking
If client has DV arrest history and FBI trafficking report:

**Structure**: Lead with trafficking-specific FBI cooperation, then briefly address DV arrest as secondary issue.

**Example**: "I reported the trafficking to the Federal Bureau of Investigation on [date]. [Brief description of cooperation]. I also want to disclose that I was arrested for [DV charge] in [year]. This arrest occurred [context: while I was still being trafficked / during an altercation with the abuser]. [Brief explanation of trafficking context]. Since coming into safety, I have [not been involved in further incidents / sought counseling / understanding of trauma]. I remain committed to cooperating with law enforcement on the trafficking investigation."

---

## Content Boundaries

### DO INCLUDE:
- Which law enforcement agency (FBI, local police, ICE, etc.)
- Date of initial report
- Type of cooperation provided
- FBI letter date
- Willingness for continued cooperation
- Altruistic motivation
- Brief explanation if cooperation was delayed

### DO NOT INCLUDE:
- Detailed evidence inventory
- Names of co-perpetrators (save for Trafficking section)
- Complex legal case status
- Investigative details
- Immigration case details or pending charges
- Extensive description of prior police interactions
- Future expectations or requests from law enforcement

## Common Scenarios & Language

### Scenario 1: Reported to FBI, Full Cooperation
"I reported the trafficking to the Federal Bureau of Investigation on [date], and I have cooperated fully with their investigation. I have provided detailed statements about my trafficking experience and about [trafficker's name]. On [date], I received a letter from the FBI confirming this cooperation. I remain willing to work with law enforcement and to testify if necessary. I reported because I want [trafficker] to be held accountable and to prevent him from trafficking others."

### Scenario 2: Local Police, Then Referred to FBI
"I initially reported the trafficking to [local police department] on [date]. They referred my case to the Federal Bureau of Investigation. I have been cooperating with the FBI since [date] and received a letter confirming this cooperation on [date]. I am willing to continue working with federal investigators and to provide any additional information that could help stop [trafficker] from harming others."

### Scenario 3: Delayed Report Due to Fear
"I did not report the trafficking immediately. I was afraid of [consequence: deportation, retaliation, not being believed, etc.]. It was not until [person/circumstance: I escaped / an advocate helped me / I realized I could receive help] that I found the courage to report to [agency name] on [date]. Since then, I have cooperated fully and remain willing to assist in any investigation. I want to come forward now to protect other victims and to ensure [trafficker] is held responsible."

### Scenario 4: No Report Yet, But Willing
"I have not yet reported the trafficking to law enforcement, but I am willing to do so. I was afraid, and I did not know how to report safely. Now I understand that I can receive protection through the T visa process. I am ready to cooperate with any law enforcement investigation into [trafficker's] crimes. I want justice and I want to prevent [trafficker] from harming others."

## Length Guidelines

- **Ideal**: 150-250 words
- **Absolute maximum**: 300 words (this is not a detailed narrative section)

This section is intentionally brief. It answers the LEA cooperation question clearly without extensive elaboration.
