# Physical Presence Section Instructions

## Format Rules
- **Paragraph count**: 1-2 paragraphs
- **Word count**: 150-300 words (HARD FAIL if exceeds 350)
- **Numbering**: Continue sequential numbering from Trafficking section
- **Heading**: No section heading

## Required Elements (ALL must be present)

1. **Direct connection statement** — explicit language linking presence to trafficking
   - "My presence in the United States is directly connected to..."
   - "I am in the United States because..."
   - "I would not be in the United States if not for..."

2. **HOW trafficking kept client in US** — explain mechanism
   - Document control preventing departure
   - Threats preventing departure
   - Debt bondage preventing departure
   - Physical confinement
   - Isolation preventing departure
   - Fear of consequences for trying to leave

3. **WHY client didn't/couldn't leave** — explain barriers
   - No money
   - No documentation
   - No knowledge of how to escape
   - Threats about consequences if they tried
   - Fear of being deported and separated from children
   - Fear of family being harmed in country of origin

## Optional Elements (If Applicable)

- **Entered for different reason**: If client entered US on a visa for lawful purpose (work, tourism, education), briefly note this was before trafficking began
  - "I initially entered the United States on a [visa type] to [purpose]..."
  - Then explain how trafficking occurred after entry

- **Escaped/Released**: If client escaped or was released mid-trafficking, briefly note when
  - "I remained in the United States even after escaping because..."

- **Left US and returned**: If client briefly left US during trafficking period
  - "Although I left the US on [date], I returned because..." (trafficker followed, threatened, coerced return, etc.)

## Critical Language Rules

### Cause-and-Effect Mandatory
Every statement must show clear causal connection between trafficking and presence.

**WRONG**: "I am in the United States."
**RIGHT**: "I am in the United States because [trafficker] brought me here and then controlled me so I could not leave."

**WRONG**: "The trafficking happened while I was here."
**RIGHT**: "The trafficking is why I am here. I would not be here if [trafficker] had not promised me a job, brought me to the US, and then enslaved me."

### Connection to Trafficking
Open or close with explicit statement that presence is trafficking-caused.

**Example opening**: "My presence in the United States is directly connected to my trafficking. I would not be here if [trafficker] had not deceived me and brought me here."

**Example closing**: "...which is why I remain in the United States today — not by choice, but because the trafficking has left me unable to return home safely."

---

## Content Boundaries

### DO INCLUDE:
- How trafficking kept client in US
- Why client couldn't leave (document control, debt, threats, fear)
- Brief mention of original purpose if entered legally
- Escape date and circumstances if applicable
- Current status (still recovering, rebuilding life, etc.)

### DO NOT INCLUDE:
- Country conditions (belongs in Extreme Hardship section)
- Recounting trafficking events (belongs in Trafficking section)
- Life built in US, community ties (belongs in Extreme Hardship)
- Evidence inventory or legal documentation
- Detailed family separation narrative (belongs in Extreme Hardship)
- Hopes for future or plans in US

## Common Structures

### Structure 1: Simple Direct Connection (1 paragraph)
"My presence in the United States is directly connected to the trafficking. [Trafficker] promised me a job as a [position], so I agreed to come to the US. When I arrived, I was enslaved. [Specific control method prevented leaving]. Because [specific consequence of leaving was feared], I could not escape and remained trapped in the United States until [escape date]. My physical presence here is entirely a result of trafficking and the control [trafficker] exercised over me."

### Structure 2: Lawful Entry + Trafficking (1-2 paragraphs)
Paragraph 1: "I initially entered the United States legally on a [visa type] in [year]. My intention was to [lawful purpose]."

Paragraph 2: "However, [trafficker] met me [at location/through connection] and promised me [false promise]. I agreed to [what I thought I was agreeing to]. Instead, [trafficking occurred]. The trafficking is why I remained in the United States after my visa expired. I could not leave because [specific control mechanisms]. My physical presence here is a direct result of this trafficking."

### Structure 3: Escape and Return (1-2 paragraphs)
"My presence in the United States is directly connected to the trafficking. I was brought here by [trafficker] and enslaved for [timeframe]. I managed to escape in [month/year], but I could not return to my country because [reason — threat to safety, fear of return trafficking, separated from children, etc.]. My physical presence in the United States continued because leaving would have put me in greater danger."

---

## PP Forward-Looking Rule

The Physical Presence section is FORWARD-LOOKING. It does not re-narrate trafficking (that was the Trafficking section). It focuses on:
- Why client is here now (because of trafficking)
- Why client could not/cannot leave (because of trafficking control)
- What barriers prevent safe return to country of origin

---

## Specific Prohibited Content

**HARD FAIL** if includes:
- Detailed re-telling of trafficking events (summarize, reference trafficking section)
- Hopes about building life in US or dreams for future
- Friendships or community connections made in US
- Fear of returning based on general country conditions (belongs in Extreme Hardship)
- Life circumstances built in US (housing, employment secured, etc.)
- Immigration status discussion beyond how trafficking kept client here

---

## Word Count Guidance

- 150-250 words: Simple cases with clear trafficking-to-presence connection
- 250-300 words: Cases requiring brief mention of lawful entry, escape, or other conditional elements
- Over 300: Reduce and consolidate. Move country fears to EH. Remove life-building language.

---

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing Physical Presence section:
- [ ] Direct connection statement present ("directly connected to trafficking")
- [ ] HOW trafficking kept client in US explained
- [ ] WHY client didn't leave explained
- [ ] Cause-and-effect language throughout
- [ ] Under 350 words (ideally 150-300)
- [ ] No re-narration of trafficking events
- [ ] No country conditions (save for EH section)
- [ ] No life-built-in-US language
- [ ] Forward-looking tone
