# Conclusion Section Instructions

## Format Rules
- **Paragraph count**: 1 paragraph only
- **Sentence count**: 3-5 sentences MAXIMUM (HARD FAIL if exceeds 5)
- **Numbering**: Final paragraph of declaration
- **Heading**: No section heading
- **Tone**: Respectful, humble, sincere plea

---

## Required Elements (ALL must be present)

1. **Plea, not summary** — HARD FAIL if conclusion recaps trafficking details or summarizes evidence
   - This is a request for mercy/protection, not a legal summary
   - No re-narration of what came before

2. **Respectful and humble language** — choose one:
   - "I respectfully and humbly ask"
   - "I beg you to consider"
   - "I humbly request"
   - "I respectfully submit this request"

3. **Brief reaffirmation of victim status** — one clause only
   - "as a victim of trafficking"
   - "as someone who has suffered severe trafficking"
   - Do NOT elaborate; do NOT re-narrate

4. **Request for T Nonimmigrant Status protection**
   - Specify "T Nonimmigrant Status" or "T visa protection"
   - Clear ask: grant the protection, approve the petition, recognize the status

5. **Statement of truthfulness** — affirm the declaration
   - "I declare under penalty of perjury that the foregoing is true and correct"
   - "Everything I have stated is truthful"
   - "I have told the truth in this declaration"

6. **Desire to heal + commitment to law** — closing values
   - "I want to heal and rebuild my life"
   - "I am committed to following the law"
   - "I will contribute to society"

7. **Gratitude** — acknowledge those who helped or consideration being given
   - "Thank you for considering my case"
   - "I am grateful for the opportunity to share my story"

---

## Structure & Examples

### Example 1: Standard Conclusion (4 sentences)

"I respectfully and humbly ask that you grant me T Nonimmigrant Status in recognition of my victimization by trafficking. Everything I have stated in this declaration is true and correct, and I declare it under penalty of perjury. I am committed to healing from my trauma, following the law, and rebuilding my life. Thank you for considering my petition and for the protections T visa status provides to trafficking victims like me."

**Breakdown**:
1. Plea + request for T status
2. Truth statement
3. Commitment to healing + law
4. Gratitude

### Example 2: Longer Conclusion (5 sentences)

"As a victim of trafficking who has suffered greatly, I respectfully and humbly request that you grant me T Nonimmigrant Status. I declare under penalty of perjury that every statement in this declaration is truthful and accurate to the best of my knowledge. I am deeply committed to rebuilding my life, healing from trauma, and contributing lawfully to society. I have cooperated with law enforcement and will continue to do so. I am grateful for your consideration and for the opportunity to seek safety and protection in the United States."

**Breakdown**:
1. Victim reaffirmation + plea for T status
2. Truth statement
3. Commitment to healing and contribution
4. Law enforcement cooperation commitment
5. Gratitude

### Example 3: Concise Conclusion (3 sentences)

"I respectfully and humbly ask that you grant me T Nonimmigrant Status as a victim of severe trafficking. I declare under penalty of perjury that this entire declaration is true and correct. I am committed to my recovery and to contributing to society, and I am grateful for your consideration of my petition."

**Breakdown**:
1. Plea + victim status + T status request
2. Truth statement
3. Commitment + gratitude

## Critical Rules

### HARD FAIL: Summary/Re-narration
**Do NOT recount trafficking details, evidence, or arguments in conclusion.**

**WRONG**: "I was trafficked by [name] from [date] to [date]. He made me work long hours and took all my money. He threatened to deport me if I tried to leave. This is why I am requesting T visa status."

**RIGHT**: "I respectfully and humbly request T Nonimmigrant Status in recognition of my victimization by trafficking. Everything in this declaration is true, and I am committed to healing and following the law."

**Why**: The Conclusion is not the place to make final arguments. Everything was established in the body. The Conclusion is purely a request.

### One-Clause Victim Reaffirmation Only
If you reference being a victim, do it in one brief clause. Do not elaborate.

**WRONG**: "As someone who was trafficked by [trafficker] for [timeframe] and suffered [specific abuses], I request..."
**RIGHT**: "As a victim of severe trafficking, I respectfully request..."

### Humble Tone Mandatory
The Conclusion is a plea. The tone should be respectful, humble, sincere — not demanding, not argumentative, not summarizing.

**WRONG**: "I have clearly demonstrated that I was trafficked, and therefore I deserve T visa status."
**RIGHT**: "I respectfully and humbly request that you grant me T Nonimmigrant Status."

### No Future Aspirations
Do NOT discuss dreams, plans, or future goals for life in the US.

**WRONG**: "I hope to graduate college and start a business here."
**RIGHT**: "I am committed to healing and rebuilding my life."

### Truth Statement Required
Every conclusion MUST include a statement affirming truthfulness.

**Acceptable language**:
- "I declare under penalty of perjury that the foregoing is true and correct"
- "Everything I have stated in this declaration is true and accurate"
- "I have told the truth in this declaration"
- "All statements herein are truthful"

---

## Emotional Authenticity

The Conclusion can be slightly more emotionally resonant than the rest of the declaration, but authenticity is key.

**Authentic**:
- "I am grateful for the opportunity to tell my story"
- "I hope to heal and rebuild the life that was taken from me"
- "I want to be free"

**Inauthentic/Performative**:
- "I am eternally grateful for your infinite mercy"
- "I pray that you will see my worthy suffering and grant me protection"
- "I can only hope that justice will prevail"

---

## Common Errors to Avoid

1. **Making final arguments**: Conclusion is not the place for new legal points
2. **Re-narrating trafficking**: All facts were in the Trafficking section
3. **Excessive length**: Keep to 3-5 sentences
4. **Defensive tone**: This is a request, not an argument
5. **Future aspirations**: Focus on healing, not dreams
6. **Multiple victim reaffirmations**: One brief clause only
7. **Vague requests**: "Please help me" — specify "T Nonimmigrant Status"
8. **Omitting truth statement**: Every declaration must end with affirmation of truthfulness

## Sample Conclusions by Approach

### Approach 1: Simple & Direct
"I respectfully request T Nonimmigrant Status in recognition of my trafficking victimization. I declare under penalty of perjury that this declaration is true and correct. I am committed to healing and rebuilding my life, and I am grateful for your consideration of my petition."

### Approach 2: Humble & Compassionate
"As a victim of trafficking, I humbly request that you grant me T Nonimmigrant Status and the protections it provides. Every statement in this declaration is truthful and made under penalty of perjury. I am committed to my recovery, to following the law, and to helping others understand the reality of trafficking. Thank you for your consideration."

### Approach 3: Grateful & Forward-Looking
"I respectfully and humbly ask that you grant me T Nonimmigrant Status. I declare under penalty of perjury that everything stated here is true. I am grateful for the opportunity to seek safety and protection, and I am determined to heal and contribute meaningfully to my community as I rebuild my life."

### Approach 4: Focused & Sincere
"I ask for T Nonimmigrant Status as a trafficking victim who seeks safety and the opportunity to heal. I declare that this entire declaration is truthful and accurate. I am committed to my recovery and to following the law, and I am grateful for your consideration of my petition."

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing Conclusion:
- [ ] Is a plea, not a summary (no trafficking re-narration)
- [ ] Opens with respectful/humble language ("respectfully and humbly request" or equivalent)
- [ ] Includes one brief victim reaffirmation clause only
- [ ] Specifically requests "T Nonimmigrant Status" or "T visa protection"
- [ ] Includes clear statement of truthfulness (penalty of perjury)
- [ ] Mentions commitment to healing and/or following the law
- [ ] Includes gratitude element
- [ ] 3-5 sentences maximum
- [ ] Humble, sincere tone (not defensive or demanding)
- [ ] No future aspirations or life plans
- [ ] No new legal arguments or evidence
- [ ] Authentically written (not performative or over-formal)
