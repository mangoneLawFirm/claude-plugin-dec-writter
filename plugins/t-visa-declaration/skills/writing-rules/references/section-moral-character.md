# Moral Character Section Instructions

## Format Rules
- **Paragraph count**: 2-3 paragraphs
- **Numbering**: Continue sequential numbering from Extreme Hardship section
- **Heading**: No section heading

---

## Three-Paragraph Structure (Standard)

### Paragraph 1: Immigration Violations — Honest Acknowledgment

**Purpose**: Address any immigration law violations client committed honestly and directly. This demonstrates integrity and willingness to take responsibility, which actually *strengthens* moral character case.

**Required if applicable**:
- Client entered without inspection
- Client worked without authorization
- Client used fraudulent documents (if applicable)
- Client overstayed visa (if applicable)

**Tone**: Sincere remorse, not defensiveness.

**Example structure**: "I must acknowledge that I entered the United States without inspection. I did not follow the proper legal process. I sincerely apologize for this violation of law. At the time, I was desperate and believed I had no choice. However, that does not excuse my actions. I take responsibility for violating immigration law. Since coming into safety, I have cooperated fully with law enforcement and the T visa process, and I have committed to following the law."

**Critical language**:
- "I sincerely apologize" — genuine remorse, not performative
- "I take full responsibility" — ownership, not blame-shifting
- "I violated the law" — direct acknowledgment, not euphemism
- "I now understand" or "I have learned" — show growth/awareness

**What NOT to do**:
- Do NOT blame trafficking for immigration violation (it may have caused it, but client still violated law)
- Do NOT be defiant or minimize the violation
- Do NOT over-explain or make excuses
- Do NOT apologize for being trafficked (only for any law violations)

**Example if entry was traffic-caused**:
"I entered the United States without inspection. I was deceived by [trafficker] who promised me a legal job and route to entry. Instead, he brought me illegally. I take responsibility for my part in this — I should have questioned why he did not use legal channels. I sincerely apologize for violating immigration law. Since my escape, I have cooperated with law enforcement and am pursuing legal status through the T visa process."

**Tax/work authorization issue**:
- If client worked without authorization: Acknowledge this was illegal; does not need to be elaborated
- If client has tax documentation (SSN, ITIN): "I have since worked legally and paid taxes" (affirm positive)
- If client has no tax documentation: No need to mention; do not introduce new problems

---

### Paragraph 2: Positive Character — Work Ethic, Non-Violence, Honest Labor

**Purpose**: Establish that despite trafficking and law violations, client has demonstrated positive character through actions and values.

**Required elements**:
- Work ethic — client works hard, is reliable, shows up
- Non-violence — client has not engaged in violence or criminal behavior
- Honesty in labor — client does work honestly, does not steal, does not engage in fraudulent activity
- Resilience — client has overcome trafficking and is rebuilding
- Commitment to law — willingness to follow the law going forward

**Do NOT include**:
- Character witnesses (save for I-864 Affidavit of Support or separate letters)
- Specific job titles or income (focus on character, not economics)
- Detailed work history (focus on values demonstrated through work)

**Example structure**: "Since my escape from trafficking, I have rebuilt my life through honest work. I have taken several jobs and demonstrated my commitment to working hard and being reliable. I arrive on time, I do my work carefully, and I take pride in what I do. I have never stolen from any employer or engaged in dishonest behavior. Even during trafficking, when I could have retaliated or acted violently toward [trafficker], I did not. I am not a violent person. My commitment is to rebuilding my life lawfully and to contributing to society."

**Specific character qualities to highlight**:
- Reliability ("I show up on time, I fulfill my responsibilities")
- Honesty ("I have never stolen, I have never lied to authorities")
- Hard work ("I work long hours, I take on difficult tasks, I do not complain")
- Non-violence ("Despite what was done to me, I have not harmed anyone")
- Resilience ("I have overcome trafficking and am rebuilding despite hardship")

---

### Paragraph 3: Character Traits — Compassion, Humility, Community

**Purpose**: Show client's values and character beyond the legal/work sphere. Demonstrate humanity and positive contribution to community.

**Optional elements** (include if genuine):
- Compassion — caring for others who have suffered, helping other victims, supporting family members
- Humility — recognition of own errors, willingness to learn, acceptance of help
- Community involvement — helping at shelter, volunteering (if applicable), supporting family in country of origin
- Spiritual/moral values — faith, ethical beliefs, commitment to doing right
- Support of family — commitment to helping family members, care for dependents
- Mentorship or helping others — specifically helping other trafficking victims if applicable

**Do NOT include**:
- Character witnesses (save for separate documentation)
- Vague claims ("I am a good person") without evidence
- Religious conversion as sole character evidence (if faith is relevant, frame as grounding for values)
- Future aspirations ("I hope to become X")

**Example structure**: "Beyond my work, I am committed to my family and my community. [If applicable: I have volunteered at a shelter for trafficking victims / I have helped other survivors navigate the legal process / I support my family members in my home country and send money to help them]. [If applicable: My faith in [religion] teaches me that I have a responsibility to help others and to live ethically]. I am humble about my past mistakes and grateful for the help I have received. I recognize that I was a victim of trafficking, but I also recognize that I violated immigration law by entering without inspection. I am committed to following the law going forward and to being a positive contributor to my community."

**Alternative framing** (for client focused on family):
"I am deeply committed to my family. Despite the trauma I have experienced, I focus on supporting the people I love. I send money to my family in [country] to help them survive. I care for my [family member] in the United States who depends on me. These commitments — to my family and to my values — guide my actions every day."

## Two-Paragraph Structure (Streamlined)

If 3 paragraphs feel redundant or if client has limited work history due to trafficking:

**Structure**:
- ¶1: Immigration violations (honest acknowledgment) + Work ethic (positive character)
- ¶2: Community/character traits + Commitment to law going forward

**Example combined ¶1**: "I must acknowledge that I entered the United States without inspection, and I sincerely apologize for violating immigration law. Since my escape from trafficking, I have rebuilt my life through honest work. I am reliable, I work hard, and I take pride in doing my work well. I have never stolen or engaged in dishonest behavior. I am not a violent person, and despite the terrible treatment I suffered, I did not retaliate with violence. I am committed to following the law going forward."

## Critical Rules

### Waiver Connection
If client needs moral character waiver (due to immigration violations or other issues), connect Moral Character section to law enforcement cooperation.

**Language**: "I have cooperated fully with law enforcement in their investigation of [trafficker]. This cooperation demonstrates my commitment to the law and to holding perpetrators accountable. I recognize my past error in entering without inspection, but I am now committed to lawful conduct and to working within the legal system."

### Tax Information
- **If client has taxes on file**: Mention ("I have paid taxes on my income")
- **If client does not have tax documentation**: Do NOT mention; do not introduce new problems

### Trafficking As Context, Not Excuse
Client can acknowledge that trafficking affected their judgment or circumstances, but trafficking does not excuse immigration violations.

**Example**: "While I was trafficked, I had no control over my documents or my work. However, I take responsibility for my decision to enter the country without authorization. That was my choice, even if it was made under desperate circumstances."

---

## Content Boundaries

### DO INCLUDE:
- Immigration law violations (if any)
- Sincere apology for violations
- Work ethic and reliability
- Non-violence
- Honest labor/non-criminal behavior
- Community support or family support (if genuine)
- Commitment to law going forward
- Character values (humility, resilience, compassion)
- Law enforcement cooperation (connection to waiver)

### DO NOT INCLUDE:
- Character witnesses (separate documentation)
- Detailed work history or job titles
- Financial information or income
- Family connections as character evidence (save for I-864)
- Religious conversion as sole character evidence
- Future aspirations or dreams
- Detailed trauma narrative (belongs in Trafficking section)
- Defensive tone
