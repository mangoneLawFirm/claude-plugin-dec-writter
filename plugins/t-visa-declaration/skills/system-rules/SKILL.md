# System Rules — T-Visa Declaration Pipeline
## (7 Attorney Feedback Rounds)

---

## CRITICAL RULES (Case-Killing if Violated)

### VAWA-vs-TRAFFICKING FILTER
Every trafficking paragraph must connect MEANS (force/fraud/coercion) to ENDS (labor/servitude/financial exploitation). A paragraph that only describes emotional abuse, jealousy, controlling behavior, or insults WITHOUT tying them to labor/servitude/financial exploitation reads as domestic violence (VAWA), not trafficking. This destroys the T-visa case.
- Common VAWA patterns to reframe: "She insulted me and made me feel small" → add what labor/service the insults enforced; "I sent my location to avoid problems" → reframe as labor/service monitoring; surveillance framed as jealousy → reframe as labor/earnings monitoring

### MEANS-ENDS MANDATORY PAIRING
Every paragraph describing Means (threats, force, coercion) must explicitly connect to a specific End (labor, servitude, financial exploitation). Orphaned Means = VAWA, not trafficking.

### COERCED COMPLIANCE
All of the client's compliance must be framed as fear-driven, never voluntary or peace-seeking.
- NEVER: "my goal was to keep the peace" | "I told her it was okay" | "I wanted to avoid problems"
- ALWAYS: fear of specific consequences (deportation, physical harm, destitution)

### FIRST-PERSON SINGULAR ONLY
The declaration must use "I" and "me" exclusively for victim experience. NEVER "us/we/our" for worker group. "We" acceptable ONLY for family unit. HARD FAIL if violated.

### FIRING FEAR PROHIBITION
NEVER frame victim's fear as fear of being fired or losing a job. This implies voluntary employment and destroys the trafficking argument. Fear must be about immigration consequences, physical harm, or total destitution. HARD FAIL if violated.
- NEVER: "afraid they would fire me" | "scared of losing the job" | "afraid of being let go"

### ENFORCEMENT MECHANISM REQUIRED (ELEVATED — CRITICAL)
Every forced action must specify HOW compliance was enforced: what threats were made, what consequences the victim believed would occur. "She forced me to clean" without explaining HOW = FAIL.
- Wrong: "She forced me to cook" | Right: "She threatened to call immigration if I refused, so I cooked..."

### ENGLISH-ONLY LANGUAGE
Entire declaration must be in English. All quotes translated. No Spanish words, exclamations, or parenthetical terms. HARD FAIL if any Spanish text remains.

## ACTIVE VOICE AND LANGUAGE RULES

### ACTIVE VOICE
Trafficker must be the grammatical subject of action verbs. Never passive.
- WRONG: "I was required to cook" | RIGHT: "Carmelo forced me to cook"
- WRONG: "I was taken to Charlotte" | RIGHT: "Carmelo took me to Charlotte"

### SEXUAL VIOLENCE LANGUAGE
NEVER "sexually abused," "sexual assault," or clinical terms. ALWAYS "sexual slave," "sexual servitude," "forced into sexual servitude," "forced himself on me."

### PLAIN LANGUAGE
No English idioms that sound like Spanglish/literal translations.
- WRONG: "to get me in the door" | RIGHT: "to convince me to start working there"
- WRONG: "they had me over a barrel" | RIGHT: "they knew I had no other options"

### THREATS IN QUOTES
Default to direct quoted speech for trafficker's threats. All quotes in English.

## STRUCTURAL RULES

### PARAGRAPH OPENING VARIETY
Max 2-3 trafficking paragraphs may have the trafficker as subject of the FIRST MAIN CLAUSE (counts even if brief prep phrase precedes: "After I moved in, Vanessa..." still counts). Open with victim's experience, emotion, pattern/technique, or full contextual scene instead. Trafficker remains active subject WITHIN paragraph body.

### BACKGROUND LIMIT
STRICTLY 2-3 paragraphs. Every sentence must pass vulnerability test. No 3rd paragraph without attorney override.

### BACKGROUND BOUNDARY — US-MET TRAFFICKERS
When trafficker was met in the US: Background ¶2 must NOT establish US desperation ("willing to take any job," "no connections," "afraid to complain"). This frames case as VAWA. Vulnerability comes from trafficker's scheme, not pre-existing desperation.

### BACKGROUND NO INTENT TO REMAIN
No language suggesting intent to remain permanently in US. Entry: approximate date + method only.

### FORWARD REFERENCE PROHIBITION
Never use "the [X]" (definite article) or characterize as "constant/ongoing/repeated" before the concept has been concretely introduced. Establish facts first, THEN characterize the pattern.

## TRAFFICKING SECTION RULES

### FIRST TRAFFICKING PARAGRAPH = PROCESS/SETUP
Opens with scheme paragraph: who trafficker was, relationship, how they recruited, what was PROMISED, why victim agreed (framed around PROMISES not victim's needs), how initial agreement became a hook. Does NOT describe exploitation.

### AGREEMENT FRAMING
Frame victim's agreement around trafficker's PROMISES, not victim's needs or desperation.
- NEVER: "I needed the work badly" | "I was desperate" | "I had no choice but to accept"
- ALWAYS: what the trafficker promised/deceived

### SURVEILLANCE AS TRAFFICKING TOOL
Reframe monitoring/surveillance as labor/service control, not relationship jealousy.
- WRONG: "she monitored me because she was jealous"
- RIGHT: "she required me to send my location to verify I was at work and earning money for her"

### FINANCIAL EXPLOITATION FRAMING
Trafficker as active agent forcing payment. Include amounts, items paid for, % of salary, work hours. Never "I paid for everything."

### WAGE EXPLOITATION FORMULA
When pay fraud: hours worked × promised rate = expected pay → compare to actual pay. Math proves fraud.

### CONTROL MECHANISM ESTABLISHMENT
Each control method must explain HOW the trafficker gained that control (demanded? stolen? threatened?).

### THREAT IMPACT ELABORATION
Threats must describe the FEAR they produced and the specific consequences victim believed would happen.

### THREAT TRANSMISSION SPECIFICITY
Immigration threats must be specific scenes: WHO told client, WHEN, WHAT exact words. NOT "everyone knew" or ambient atmosphere.

### ESCAPE BARRIERS
Explicitly explain WHY victim couldn't: contact family / escape when alone / approach authorities. Don't leave this implicit.

### SCENE-GROUNDING
At least one specific trigger scene per major control method. Avoid abstract-only descriptions.

### CONVERSATIONAL AUTHENTICITY
3-5 natural speech markers in the trafficking section (memory anchors, hedging, self-corrections).

### CHILD SEPARATION FRAMING
Parent wanted child but was UNABLE to take custody due to trafficking circumstances. Never sounds voluntary.

### SERVITUDE CONTRAST
Domestic servitude paragraphs must contrast trafficker's idleness with victim's forced labor.

### RECRUITMENT INFORMATION EXTRACTION
Show how trafficker learned victim's vulnerabilities (asked about traumas, family situation, etc.) under pretense of genuine interest.

## SECTION-SPECIFIC BREVITY RULES

### PP BREVITY
1-2 paragraphs, 150-300 words. Only trafficking-nexus content. No country fears. No re-narration of trafficking incidents.
- PP CAUSAL NARRATIVE: Required → direct connection statement + HOW trafficking kept client in US + WHY didn't leave + cause-and-effect language

### EH CONCISENESS
3 paragraphs max (4 only with extensive medical), 250-400 words. Safety+Country merged into 1 paragraph. Forward-looking only (removal consequences, not trafficking re-narration).
- MUST CITE: INA § 101(a)(15)(T)(i)(IV) and 8 C.F.R. § 214.209
- Country conditions paragraph: REQUIRED, not optional

### LEA BREVITY
1 concise paragraph. Reporting status + willingness + altruistic motivation. Explain delayed cooperation if applicable.

### CONCLUSION BREVITY
3-5 sentences. Plea, not summary. "I beg you" / "I respectfully and humbly ask." No recap of case details.

### GLOBAL CONCISENESS
State facts ONCE. No multi-angle restatement. No argumentative analysis. No retrospective legal framing. No pattern labeling. Total target: 2,500-3,500 words.

---

## INSULT AND VERBAL ABUSE RULES

### INSULT CONTENT SPECIFICITY
State actual insults or their specific nature. Never just "insults," "degrading language," or "foul language" without specifics. If unknown, flag [ATTORNEY REVIEW: Confirm specific insults used].
